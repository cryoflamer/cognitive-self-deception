# Core theses

## Main thesis

Intelligence does not necessarily reduce self-deception. Under motivated reasoning, it can optimize self-deception by making it more complex, stable, and rhetorically convincing.

## Secondary theses

1. Arguments can be secondary to psychologically comfortable conclusions.
2. Reflection can become selective: sharp toward others, protective toward the self.
3. Critical theories can be inverted into defensive mechanisms.
4. Higher cognitive resources can stabilize beliefs when accuracy motivation is weaker than identity protection.
5. Rational criticism is structurally limited when arguments serve a defensive function.

## Key concepts

- Motivated reasoning: reasoning directed toward a desired conclusion.
- Rationalization: post hoc logical justification of a position or behavior.
- Intellectualization: abstract theorizing used to distance oneself from emotional conflict.
- Selective reflection: applying critical analysis outward while limiting it inward.
- Metacognitive rupture: the failure of reflection to reflect on its own premises.
